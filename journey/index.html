Professional path

How I became the builder I am.

A chronological story of becoming a software developer, then a senior builder, founder, technology leader and AI product maker in financial services.

Build Idea Tool
  1. 1996

    First useful software

    Self-taught Visual Basic developer

    Before the financial systems and leadership roles, there was a practical little application for managing cars, motorcycles, service history, receipts and maintenance planning. It was the first proof that software could turn a real-life annoyance into something useful.

    ToolIdea
  2. 1998-2001

    Computer networks foundation

    University of Westminster, School of Computer Science

    A 1st Class Honours degree in Computer Communications and Networks gave the curiosity a serious technical base: systems, networks, communication protocols and the discipline to understand how software behaves beyond one machine.

    EducationTool
  3. 2001

    From curiosity to front office

    Beauchamp Financial Technology - Software Developer, Core Services

    After building the MP3 Home Player, Matt joined Beauchamp Financial Technology, a financial software company serving hedge funds. The work moved quickly from personal invention into professional trade capture, core products and the front-office realities of investment technology.

    BuildWorkTool
  4. 2002-2004

    Web, data and trading workflows

    Beauchamp Financial Technology - Senior Software Developer, Front Office

    The toolkit expanded through Oracle, PL/SQL, HTML, JavaScript, source control, C++, SQLite and design patterns. The responsibility expanded with it: web trade capture, consolidated blotters and TradeManager, an enterprise front-office platform for rapid trade capture, FIX and workflow management.

    ToolBuild
  5. 2005-2007

    Software becomes trading edge

    Marble Bar Asset Management - Senior Software Developer

    At Marble Bar Asset Management, a London long/short equity hedge fund, Matt helped build RAID: an integrated research, portfolio, risk and black-box trading platform. It became part of the firm's decision-making process and contributed to AUM growth from $800m to $3b.

    WorkBuild
  6. 2007-2008

    Founder mode begins

    Capital Coders Ltd - Founder

    Capital Coders began as a technology start-up and became a UK financial software provider for hedge funds. The work ranged from TraderAnalytics and portfolio risk tooling to Reuters-backed option chain applications, VBA add-ins and bespoke systems for firms including Tisbury Capital and Linedata Services.

    WorkIdeaBuild
  7. 2009-2010

    Building the global platform

    BTG Pactual - Senior Software Developer to Head of Development

    BTG Pactual, an investment bank, asset manager and wealth manager, hired Matt early into its London build-out. The role started hands-on, getting the asset-management business moving, then grew into building international development capability and systems such as trade reconciliation, DB2Email and HFT monitoring.

    WorkBuild
  8. 2011-2012

    From builder to systematic trader

    BTG Pactual - Systematic Trader

    This chapter pulled software directly into trading. Matt co-designed and back-tested a multi-portfolio, multi-asset model, then built the infrastructure to run it end to end. The strategy returned 10% during a period of extreme market uncertainty.

    WorkBuild
  9. 2012-2014

    Leadership and product thinking

    BTG Pactual - Head of IT Development/Delivery, New York, London, Brazil and Hong Kong

    The role widened into international delivery across asset management, broker dealer, re-insurance, investment banking and wealth management. Alongside the day job came a sharper product imagination: API of You, EstateBox and OrbitThis, plus Harvard Business School leadership training.

    WorkIdeaEducation
  10. 2015-2016

    Architecture at scale

    BTG Pactual - Global Head of Commodities IT; HEDGD Limited - Head of Technology

    At BTG Pactual, Matt oversaw global commodities technology across 31 offices in 18 countries, with a team of 70 and a $25m+ annual IT spend. The next move was HEDGD Limited, leading a multi-asset SaaS OMS built with C#, ASP.NET, SQL Server, FIX, CQRS, event sourcing and native iOS workflows.

    ToolBuildWork
  11. 2019-2020

    Workflow products sharpen

    HEDGD Limited to Harkster Limited - Product and technology lead

    The product focus tightened around communication and decision flow. Order Chat gave portfolio managers and execution traders a cleaner workflow, FinTiq refined channel-based notifications, and MicroReports became the bridge into Harkster's research-led future.

    BuildWork
  12. NOW
    2023-NOW

    AI-native research systems

    Harkster Limited - Head of Technology

    Harkster brings the whole path together: financial research, product judgement, architecture, trading context and hands-on engineering. Matt architected and developed a multi-tenant RAG research email client with chunking, summarization, semantic search, trade idea generation and AI assistants for institutional research workflows.

    BuildToolNow
